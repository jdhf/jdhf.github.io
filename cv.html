INTERESTS

Galaxy evolution, with particular emphasis in quantifying the impact of the different environmental processes (e.g. gas stripping, starvation, harassment, tidal interactions) on the galaxy properties (star-formation activity, morphology, etc) and the interplay among galaxy components (stellar populations, ISM, dust). My work has been focused on the analysis of spectroscopic and broad-band data of galaxy populations from the UV to the FIR, including the optical and NIR spectral ranges.

2005-2009Ph.D. fellowship, PhD Thesis Title: “Environmental influence in the star formation activity of galaxies in a sample of nearby clusters”, IAA-CSIC, Spain, Aug 2005 - Jun 2009 & Sep 2009 - Dec 2009.
ISBN: 978-84-694-2909-9
Supervisors: Prof. J. M. Vilchez and Dr. J. Iglesias-Paramo
Summary: We analize several aspects of the influence of the environment on the star formation activity of a sample of ∼5000 galaxies in 16 clusters at z<0.05. The clusters were selected to be observed by the main galaxy surveys from UV to FIR (i.e. GALEX, SDSS, 2MASS, IRAS) down to MB∼-18 throughout several Mpc in physical size. We developed a set of SEDs obtained with the spectral synthesis code GRASIL in order to derive the properties (SFR, M∗) from our galaxy sample. The PhD Thesis resulted in three articles accepted in ApJS, ApJ and MNRAS.

We derive and describe the first Hα observations and star formation activity of 11 SFCGs. Those groups present the largest physical concentrations of star-formation activity in the Local Universe.

We describe a newly identified sample of Star-Forming Compact Groups which presents a combination of properties such as low velocity dispersions (and virial masses), small dynamical time-scales and high star-forming fraction, clearly distinct from compact group samples studied up to now.
